What is coalbed methane?

Coalbed methane is natural gas adsorbed onto the internal surfaces of coal. Unlike conventional gas, it is held in place by reservoir pressure rather than a structural trap — production works by pumping water out of the coal seam to lower pressure, which releases the gas.

Adsorbed, not free

Over 90% of the gas is adsorbed in coal micropores. Recovery requires depressurization through sustained dewatering, which is why CBM wells ramp up slowly rather than peaking on day one.

Low permeability

Chinese CBM reservoirs are typically high-rank coals with permeability below 1 mD. Hydraulic fracturing and horizontal drilling are essential to achieve commercial flow rates.

Clean and dual-purpose

Extracting methane before or during coal mining reduces mine explosion risk and captures a potent greenhouse gas, turning a safety hazard into pipeline-quality fuel.

The Qinshui Basin, Shanxi

Located in southern Shanxi Province, the Qinshui Basin is the most commercially developed CBM basin in China, hosting the Panzhuang, Fanzhuang, Zhengzhuang, and Panhe fields.

How a CBM well is developed

A modern horizontal well in the Qinshui Basin goes through four main stages from drilling to peak production.

Drilling and completion — 1 to 2 months

A horizontal lateral of 1,000–2,000 m is drilled along the coal seam and completed with casing or screen pipe. Two-string well designs and "well factory" pad drilling have cut cycles and cost substantially.

Hydraulic fracturing

Staged fracturing pumps high-pressure fluid through perforations to crack the coal, then packs the fractures with sand proppant. Modern large-volume designs use 2,000 m³ of fluid and 150 m³ of sand per stage at 15 m³/min.

Dewatering — 3 to 8 months

Pumps lift water from the seam to lower reservoir pressure below the gas desorption threshold. Gas typically appears within 1–3 months in good blocks, but production is minimal during this phase.

Ramp-up to peak — 6 to 18 months from spud

Gas rate climbs as the depressurized area expands. Modern mid-deep horizontal wells have reached 18,000+ m³/d, followed by a multi-year stable production plateau.
